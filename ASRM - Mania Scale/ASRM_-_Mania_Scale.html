Results for ASRM-data (Mania Scale)

Description:

The data is from the Altman Self-Rating Mania Scale (ASRM) which was used by Nicolai & Moshagen (2018) as a possible control variable in a multiple regression model that quantified the association between pathological buying and the judgement of elapsed time. The ASRM consists of five 0-4 Likert-scored response items and was filled out by 78 participants.

Variables:

  • asrm_1: I feel more happy and cheerful than usual
  • asrm_2: I feel mor self-confident than usual
  • asrm_3: I need less sleep than usual
  • asrm_4: I talk more than usual
  • asrm_5: I am more active than usual

The example demonstrates a Bayesian unidimensional reliability analysis with coefficients omega and alpha to see how large the reliability of the unidimensional scale is.

References:

Nicolai, J., & Moshagen, M. (2018). Pathological buying symptoms are associated with distortions in judging elapsed time. Journal of Behavioral Addictions, 7 (3), 752–759. doi:10.1556/2006.7.2018.80

Conclusions:

The reliability estimated by omega is somewhere between mediocre and good. The 95% credible interval ranges from .69 to .851, meaning it is 95% likely omega is between those values. The "magical" cutoff of .7 is not that clearly exceeded.

The deletion of item 3 would result in a higher reliability if we just look at the point estimates. If we look at the interval estimates in Table 2, we see that the omega credible interval would actually indicate a slightly smaller reliability (the difference is so small that it is probably not meaningful though)

The prior probability of alpha being larger than .7 (and smaller than 1) is .246. After seeing the data the posterior probability of alpha being larger than .7 is .978.
